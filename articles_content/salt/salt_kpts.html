Movements
Osmosis is the rapid movement of water towards salt. It affects ingredient texture by damaging the cells
Diffusion is the slow movement of the water, now salted, travels back through the ingredient. It is important to achieve seasoning throughout a large ingredient and may hours to days to be complete
Higher amounts of water, higher temperatures, agitation, low ratio of salt to water and smaller salt grains all contribute to faster dissolution and diffusion of salt
There is no way to remove salt from a dish once it has dissolved
