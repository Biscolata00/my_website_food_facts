## Wet Brining
* * *

The goal of wet brining is to **increase the water content of the meat** so that the cooked product ends up moister and becomes generally more difficult to overcook.

### Technique
To wet brine, add your raw meat to water that contains 5% salt.
Let brine for 30 minutes up to a few hours, depending on the size of your ingredients.

### How it works
The salt is the main element here, as it will relax the proteins, thus allowing it to absorb more water -the proteins relaxing also allow for more tender meat, and salting that way helps the whole chunk to get salted. Through osmosis, salt draws water into the meat's cells, increasing the meat's overall liquid content.
Because osmosis is driven by reestablishing balance, the more you create an imbalance and up the percentage of salt, the faster the salt will reach the deeper in your chunks. On the other hand, salt your brine over 10%, and you risk turning your meat overly salty.

### Notes
You should not brine the skin: when cooking skin, we aim to make it crispy, which is done by removing water. Increasing the skin’s water content would thus go against this and leave you with a soggy, unpalatable skin.
